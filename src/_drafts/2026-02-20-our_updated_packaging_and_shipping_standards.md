We’ve updated our shipping standards. Plants are living things, not warehouse inventory. If they’re going to spend time in a box, that environment needs to make sense for them. These updates are about control, clarity, and better outcomes on arrival.

<!--more-->

You won’t see flashy tape all over the outside. Boxes remain clean and professional. Inside, however, you’ll notice orange tape. It’s placed intentionally, with labeled pull tabs and notes so you know exactly where and how to open the package. No guessing. No digging around with scissors near your plant.

There will also be a Tiger Lily Plants LLC sticker on the outside of the box for identification. That’s it. It’s there so you know it’s from us the moment it arrives. Clear source. No confusion.

For items that require high humidity for the duration of their life, we’ve upgraded containment. These plants are shipped in plastic cups packed with sphagnum moss. Sphagnum retains moisture while allowing airflow, and the enclosed cup maintains a stable humid microclimate during transit. This reduces shock and dehydration significantly.

Shipping live plants will always involve variables, but the packing shouldn’t be one of them. Internal orange tape with clear tabs. Exterior identification sticker. Humidity-controlled cups when required. Practical improvements that prioritize plant health first.

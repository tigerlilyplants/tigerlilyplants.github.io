### Potted Orchids

![My first epiphytic orchid bloom](/images/2026-06-11/My-first-epiphytic-orchid-bloomed-for-the-first-time-this-past-January.jpeg)

This was my first orchid, it's what got me into the hobby in the first place!

I learned that the roots tell all: whether an orchid is healthy, underwatered, or overwatered. It's easy to discern the state of a plant in this manner because the roots poking out of the media will be a silvery-gray and start to shrink when the velamen (the spongy outer layer of the roots that holds and absorbs moisture) is starting to dry out.

When the velamen dries out, depending on the species of orchid, this can lead to the rest of the plant's decay; some plants are easily affected by dryness or low humidity, like the Lepanthes I included at the outset of this post, and require constant moisture.

Phalanopsis orchids tend to be fine with periods of dryness as they have thick roots. My general rule of thumb for growing is, the smaller the roots, the more frequently I water and the lower the concentration of fertilizer, roughly 1/2-1/8th the typical strength.

#### Coconut orchids

![Orchid bloom 2](/images/2026-06-11/11C6BEA7-02CE-4754-8789-5C3B150C05FD_1_105_c.jpeg)
*A coconut orchid that's re-bloomed for me after 2 years of new growth.*

Coconut orchids are relatively easy to grow. I tend to keep the roots moist for the most part, with short periods of dryness. This is the first coconut orchid that has rebloomed for me after about 2 years of new growth. In that time, it's nearly doubled in size as bulbs have come in. I originally bought this at Logee's in Connecticut when I lived in Massachusetts.

#### Gastrochilus

![Orchid bloom 5](/images/2026-06-11/290DC346-365C-43EE-B14E-EBD5EB416FF0_1_105_c.jpeg)
*A gastrochilus in bloom. I got this plant recently from Rainbeau Orchids.*

Gastrochilus like to stay wet in my experience. I bought this particular orchid from Rainbeau, which is a greenhouse I frequent.

> I have [Gastrochilus Hoi](https://www.etsy.com/listing/4494456036/gastrochilus-hoii-mini-orchid) on my storefront if you'd like to give one a try.

#### Dendrochilum

![Orchid bloom 6](/images/2026-06-11/2CF8E423-9E92-4801-BBE6-887D142EF3A5_1_102_o.jpeg)
*Dendrochilum tenellum.*

Similar to many of the other orchids here, I keep dendrochilums' roots moist. They're hardly as thick as phalanopsis roots and the bulbs tend to start to shrink from dehydration after only a couple days at ambient humidity if they are not kept wet.

That said, they do relatively well at ambient humidity.

#### Bulbophyllums

![Bulbophyllums](/images/2026-06-11/5EE036A4-7850-4F20-8D70-50184717E2BF_1_105_c.jpeg)
*A couple bulbophyllums in my collection that bloomed at the same time:)*

I've never lost a bulbophyllum. They make excellent beginner orchids for those who don't mind watering them every other day or so. They remind me a lot of dendrochilums in terms of care.

The left one I bought from Rainbeau Orchids, again, and it has bloomed 3-4 times since then, sometimes even two spikes at the same time!

![Bulbophyllums](/images/2026-06-11/9E0B4164-D4E4-4CCD-80F7-2A1DB80CD438_1_105_c.jpeg)

These plants are, again, super easy to grow in my experience and appreciate daily watering or >80% humidity.

Getting these to bloom in my experience does require higher humidity. Otherwise you're likely just to get leaves.

### Terrarium blooms

#### My first terrarium

My first terrarium, which may be viewed in an earlier post, was an experiment. I wanted to be able to control the humidity in a closed space with a controller to avoid daily watering. This terrarium has been going strong now for nearly a year.

![Begonia vankerckhovenii](/images/2026-06-11/8B783F83-C524-4319-BF1A-645D95E5C037_1_105_c.jpeg)

Begonia vankerckhovenii is an easy bloomer, but its large yellow flowers never fail to impress.

![Bulbophyllum dennisii](/images/2026-06-11/55FA7576-656E-4038-B9B1-8D121FDD8D08_1_105_c.jpeg)

bulbophyllum Dennisii has bloomed for me only once since I fixed it to the log. However, it has seen impressive, tight growth. I expected the flowers to be a bit more pinkish, but they are indeed a stark yellow.

![Orchid bloom 11](/images/2026-06-11/3870307D-5AE5-4EC4-AE43-A4DE0C9C9B88_1_105_c.jpeg)

#### Hexagonal terrarium

This is my most recent build and I wrote a lengthy post about the species I originally planted back in [January](/2026/01/19/orchidarium-build/). I've since added a branch and many species of Lepanthes, Dendrobiums and Platysteles.

![Orchid bloom 13](/images/2026-06-11/5D227A48-9D58-4B6D-ADCA-A29A1A451D99_1_102_a.jpeg)

I've also seen quite a few blooms from the carnivorous plants I added. Both Utricularias have bloomed at least twice in the past ~5-6 months and the Marcgravias have only put out more and more leaves.

![Orchid bloom 9](/images/2026-06-11/36E857C2-FCCE-48D6-AEEC-028688EB4B1D_1_102_a.jpeg)
*A carnivorous plant in bloom.*

Shown below is a Psygmorchis Pusilla, which has had a large, long-lasting yellow flower. My Lepanthes Calodictyon has been blooming almost continuously as well, which is usually a sign that this one is happy.

> I have Psygmorchis Pusillas for sale on my [store front](https://www.etsy.com/listing/4483006882/psygmorchis-pusilla-mini-orchid) as well:)

![Orchid bloom 10](/images/2026-06-11/3818DF58-97AA-46E6-8459-06DE6C181621_1_105_c.jpeg)

I love Lepanthes (they may even be my favorite orchid genus), but they can be difficult to care for.

![Orchid bloom 1](/images/2026-06-11/07459B6D-C2E9-42B8-A9FD-7BDEC8F79B27_1_105_c.jpeg)

A Lepanthes Gargoyla in bloom. This one has been relatively easy to care for, but I am concerned that the moss will eventually outgrow the plant. Unlike Calodictyon, this Lepanthes does not appear to be a continuous bloomer.
